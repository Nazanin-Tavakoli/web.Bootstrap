Types of Book genres

Fantasy

Books that involve elements of magic,
supernatural, or mythological creatures and settings.
Examples: Harry Potter by J.K. Rowling, The Lord of
the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien, The Chronicles of Narnia
by C.S. Lewis.

Read More

Science Fiction

Books that explore futuristic or technological
themes and ideas, often based on scientific principles or speculations.
Examples: The Hunger Games by Suzanne Collins, The Martian by Andy Weir,
1984 by George Orwell.

Read More
Romance

Books that focus on the relationship
and romantic love between two or more characters,
usually with a happy ending. Examples: Pride and
Prejudice by Jane Austen, The Notebook by Nicholas
Sparks, Fifty Shades of Grey by E.L. James.

Read More
Mystery

Books that involve a crime, a puzzle,
or a secret that needs to be solved by the characters,
often with suspense and clues. Examples: Sherlock Holmes
by Arthur Conan Doyle, The Girl on the Train by Paula Hawkins,
And Then There Were None by Agatha Christie.

Read More
Thriller

Books that create a feeling of excitement,
danger, or suspense for the readers, often involving action,
violence, or twists. Examples: The Da Vinci Code by Dan Brown,
Gone Girl by Gillian Flynn, The Bourne Identity by Robert Ludlum.

Read More
Horror

Books that intend to scare, shock, or disturb the
readers, often involving supernatural, paranormal,
or psychological elements. Examples: The Shining by
Stephen King, Dracula by Bram Stoker, The Exorcist
by William Peter Blatty.
